Schenectady Stockade and its history of flooding.

The Stockade district of Schenectady, NY is positioned next to the Mohawk river. In the last century, it has flooded 25 times. Floods are sometimes caused by ice jams in the winter, but often occur after severe storms, like Hurricane Irene. The usual height of the Mohawk river is 212 feet. Hurricane Irene and the flood of 1913 are classified as "100 year floods."
